Dr. Norman Borlaug

Norman Ernest Borlaug (March 25, 1914 â€“ September 12, 2009) was an American agronomist who led initiatives worldwide that contributed to the extensive increases in agricultural production termed the Green Revolution. Borlaug was awarded multiple honors for his work in developing high-yield, disease-resistant wheat varieties. He was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970 for his contributions to world peace through increasing food supply.

Through his efforts, Borlaug is often credited with saving over a billion people from starvation, earning him the title "The Man Who Saved A Billion Lives." His work significantly influenced agricultural practices, food security, and global development.
